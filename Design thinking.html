Design Thinking
Methodology

The thought process

Designing a website that showcases the beauty of Jeju island
was a daunting task, but through the application of design
thinking principles, I was able to create a website that balances
utility and usefulness. The biggest hurdle was identifying what
information was necessary to showcase the island's uniqueness.

Step One: Empathise and Define

To craft an effective website, I created two personas - young couples and families who love the outdoors - and crafted a user journey for them. This helped me identify what might entice them to consider Jeju island as a vacation destination. Furthermore, I interviewed young couples and families to get a better understanding of what they look for in a vacation and their thoughts on Jeju island. This provided valuable insights on keywords to use in the copy, as well as ways to better showcase events and activities on the island.

Step Two: Ideate and Prototype

To ensure that the website was user-friendly, I created low-fidelity sketches to develop the wireframes. After including the copy, I built a second draft on Figma to review the utility of the site and the user experience. Cross referencing to other sites, I also looked at how other tourism organisation showcase information on their site in order to implement the best practices by the industry for this tourism website. Furthermore, analysing the existing VisitJeju’s main page helped me better understand ways we can improve the overall usability.
